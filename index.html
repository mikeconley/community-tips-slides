How to Seem Human on the Web

There are LOTS of projects on the web

Why will someone approach yours?

Your project needs to be welcoming

  • A good README! What is this thing? What is its purpose?
  • Up to date documentation on how to get started
  • A list of introductory, mentored work that people can do
  • Communications channels for people to get help from
  • Mentors, who can build mentors, who can build mentors

It's more than documents though

Imagine a school cafeteria...

You are the Cool Kids Table

Signage helps but isn't everything

Your project needs to be human

  • People want to be cool. It’s scary to approach a new table and just dive in right away and get involved in a community because: “They might think I’m dumb”, “They might think I’m not cool”, “They might not like me"
  • Show people how you make mistakes. These are golden opportunities for humanizing yourself
  • Show yourself struggling. Show yourself failing
  • This is the soft underbelly that is approachable and human

Practical ways to humanize yourself

  • I livehack. I stumble and fall regularly. I get into trouble
  • Blog about what you're doing, the pitfalls you're hitting, and what's not working along with what is
  • Publish your notes if you have any. Show them how you try things and are wrong, and then how you find success!
  • Podcasting?

Not knowing is okay

  • Try to create a culture where saying "I don't know" is an opportunity to find things out
