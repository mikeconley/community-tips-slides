Community-building Tips

X-Bugzilla-Mentors

Engaging someone with an unknown skillset...

  • Be gentle. Probe sensitively.
  • Assume they're starting from zero, but be ready to ramp up if they show they've got a greater understanding.
  • Assume positive intent! They're nervous and want to be cool. So did you.
  • Be available. Yes, point to documentation, but also leave yourself available for questions.

    "Here - this should tell you everything you need to know" is not as good as...

    "Check this out - let me know if you have any questions!"

  • Ask questions - be curious about how they're doing, and if they're having fun.

Hand-hold, but then start to release...

  • After their first patch, point them to a new one!
  • Get them connected with other mentors as well, so they have multiple connections
  • If possible, get them into IRC and talking to the team - not just PM'ing you
  • Praise them and thank them! THEY ARE AWESOME.
  • Badges are remarkably effective! So is public praise - though perhaps emphasize praising the work they've done rather than the contributor directly to avoid possibly embarassing them.
  • Find out what they want to do, and help them get there. Introduce them to the right people.

When a contributor is off in the weeds...

  • In my experience, they usually know it.
  • It's possible that the bug they're working on isn't as interesting as they originally thought. Try offering alternatives?
  • Help them break problems down into smaller pieces.
  • Poorly spec'd solutions can be heartbreaking. Be truly confident when you send a contributor down a path - or at least warn them early if you're not 100% confident.

They belong.
